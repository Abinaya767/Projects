ABOUT THOSE COLLEGES

1. IIT Madras: The Indian Institute of Technology Madras is a premier engineering institution in India, offering undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in various engineering and technology disciplines.

2. Loyola College, Chennai: Established in 1925, Loyola College is an autonomous Jesuit institution affiliated with the University of Madras. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in arts, sciences, and commerce.

3. Madras Christian College (MCC): Founded in 1837, MCC is one of Asia's oldest extant colleges. It offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across arts, sciences, and commerce streams.

4. Women's Christian College (WCC): Established in 1915, WCC is an autonomous institution affiliated with the University of Madras. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in arts and sciences, focusing on women's education.

5. Ethiraj College for Women: Founded in 1948, Ethiraj College is an autonomous institution affiliated with the University of Madras. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in arts, sciences, and commerce, dedicated to empowering women through education.

6. Guru Nanak College: Established in 1971, Guru Nanak College is an autonomous institution located in Velachery, Chennai. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in arts, sciences, and commerce.

7. Apollo College of Arts and Science: Located in Guduvanchery, Chennai, Apollo Arts and Science College offers undergraduate programs in arts and sciences.

8. Queen Mary's College: Established in 1914, Queen Mary's College is an autonomous institution affiliated with the University of Madras. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in arts and sciences, focusing on women's education.

9. M.O.P. Vaishnav College for Women: Established in 1992, M.O.P. Vaishnav College is an autonomous institution affiliated with the University of Madras. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in commerce, management, science, and humanities, focusing on empowering women through education.
